Wushu

The Sport

Wushu is a form of Chinese martial arts that is derivative from traditional kung fu. Popular media including Avatar: The Last Airbender and Kung Fu Panda demonstrate some styles of kung fu including taichi, baguazhang, and animal forms. Wushu taolu is an international sport that applies the performance and acrobatic aspects from kung fu into contemporary forms that are dynamic, powerful, and fluid. Wushu encompasses barehand styles and weapon styles, including short, long, and soft weapons.

My Experience

I have practiced wushu since childhood. I trained under my shifu for over a decade before I left for college. In high school, I performed annually with other wushu athletes at my school's multicultural week performances.

In college, I am part of Husky Wushu at UW. Our club does performances for events held by other clubs and local organizations and competes in the annual collegiate tournament. In my first year, I earned 1 first place and 1 third place in my individual events, as well as third place in the team group set event with my teammates, the first time in collegiates history that UW placed in that event.

You can find more about Husky Wushu at our club instagram @uwwushu and website.
